    Fifa is broken.

    I have been playing every FIFA as it came out since FIFA 10, and I played many different editions of FIFA before that for many different consoles. And it seems every year, the gameplay gets worse and worse.

    I'm glad EA focuses on things other than gameplay. And I'm sure they do that for a very good reason as the game still sells well and everything. But as I want to get more into game development, I want to see which area of game development interests me. And I still haven't decided, but something that will always interest me is AI. And that's thanks to FIFA.

    Again, FIFA is broken. But I'd say it was FIFA 19 that really seemed like everything was a mess. What in my opinion has really always been broken is the AI, specifically the defending. Now, when I say broken, it's coming from someone who has played the position throughout his whole life in the sport and has also dedicated time to learning about the position in general. In reality, I bet FIFA's AI does almost exactly what EA want it to. What that is, however, is far from correct.

    Looking at the two pictures on the left, it doesn't seem like there is anything to notice. Breaking it down, the ball was just kicked down the field by the goalkeeper of the team in white. So there are two attackers (in white) and three defenders (in red). Just from a common sense point of view, it doesn't make sense that two attackers could so easily get by three defenders.

    But this is a sport after all and not math, so two beating three happens all the time. But it takes skill and thought. Here, the user just passed to his attacking teammate as usual, without thinking of how to get by the three defenders. Why? Because the defenders in FIFA take themselves out of the game. We see three defenders here, but notice the icon above on of them. Another user is controlling that defender. This situation is actually a 2v1 in favor of the attackers. This is due to the lack of knowledge by the AI defenders.

    For the furthest back defender in the top image, he obviously has no intention of playing anyone offside. So why stop when you can see the attacker is trying to play it to his teammate making the run? Stopping causes the defender to have to speed back up, obviously allowed the attacker to get in behind. That simple choice of stopping caused a goal to be let in. And that was it. That was the only reason. As a player, there is nothing more frustrating. Such a simple action caused the worst outcome. Moments like that are what made me want to become a game developer, and this moment is particular makes me think perhaps I should pursue AI.
